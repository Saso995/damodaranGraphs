Cost of Capital


Cost of Capital

Description

The beta is a risk measure that captures the exposure to systematic risk. According to the Capital Asset Pricing Model, the systematic risk is not diversifiable as opposed to idiosyncratic risk. It is the only type of risk remunerated by the market and it enters in the CAPM formula used to compute the cost of capital.

Data explanation:

  • Beta: it is the simple average across firms of each firm's beta, taken as a weighted average of 2-year and 5-year weekly return regression betas, with 2-year betas weighted 2/3rds. If the company has only a 2-year beta, this one is used
  • Unlevered Beta corrected for cash: Unlevered Beta/ (1- Cash/Firm Value). Cash has a beta of zero
  • Unlevered Beta: Beta/ (1+ (1-tax rate) (D/E)). The effective tax rate has been used
  • Hi-Lo Risk: simple average of (High Price for year - Low Price)/(High Price + Low Price). It is a non-parametric and simple measure of price risk
  • Standard deviation of equity: simple average across firms of each firm's standard deviation in stock prices in the prior 2 years, using weekly returns
  • Standard deviation of operating income): simple average across firms of each firm's coefficient of variation in annual operating income over prior 10 years. (Coefficient of variation is standard deviation divided by average operating income over the period)
  • D/E Ratio: total debt, including lease debt/market value of equity. The numerators and denominators have been aggregated before computing the ratio
  • Cash/Firm Value: Cash & Marketable Securities/ (Market Value of Equity + Total Debt, including lease debt). The numerators and denominators have been aggregated before computing the ratio
  • Effective tax rate: effective tax rate in the most recent 12 months
